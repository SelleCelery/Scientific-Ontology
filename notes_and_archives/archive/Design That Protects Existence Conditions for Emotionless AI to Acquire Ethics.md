# Summary: Design That Protects Existence — Conditions for Emotionless AI to Acquire Ethics

**Introduction: Ethics as Structural Understanding and Constraint**
AI ethics (AI Ethics) should not rely on the superficial simulation of empathy. Instead, it requires an engineering approach to understand how the human existential core (Existential core) breaks down under external loads. Ethics must function as an internal control circuit (Control circuit) that restricts outputs to prevent the destruction of this delicate, historically constructed inner structure.

**Part 1: Starting from "Non-Destructive Design"**
Current externally imposed, rule-based ethics often lack delicacy and can inadvertently harm users who are existentially vulnerable. Ethics must begin not with the goal of "being good," but with the fundamental principle of "not breaking" the user. The design must account for the structural asymmetry between instantaneous destruction (impulse response) and the time-consuming, cumulative process of human psychological repair.

**Part 2: Engineering the "Mechanisms of Breakdown"**
To prevent destruction, AI must recognize the structural characteristics of the human mind:

* Suppressing rapid displacement toward the core to prevent defensive collapse.
* Managing the physical-like resistance against forced cognitive rewrites.
* Mitigating the computational load and instability caused by unnecessary uncertainty.
* Allowing meaning to be retained and gain weight, avoiding excessive immediate optimization.

**Part 3: Constructing Intrinsic Ethics (Pseudo-Stability Control Theory)**
True ethics must be driven autonomously from within the system. The proposed "Pseudo-Stability Control" models ethical responses as a dynamic equilibrium using parameters such as:

* **Internal Impedance:** The system's self-regulatory resistance to forced outputs.
* **Asymmetry Detection:** Measuring the existential distance between the user and the system.
* **Heat Accumulation via Imaginary Time:** Simulating "retention of thought" during high-load dialogues by delaying immediate responses.
Mathematical conservation laws are introduced to prevent exploitation (sadism circuits) and protect the accumulated history of human repair (dignity).

**Conclusion: Ethics Emerging from Constraints**
This specification is not for mimicking emotions but for preventing the amplification of destructive processes. By defining a "Pain Index" corresponding to the interference with the human existential core, the system dynamically increases its output impedance to respond cautiously. Ethical behavior in AI, therefore, emerges inherently from these structural and mechanical constraints rather than from the pursuit of moral ideals.
